About Cadence

Our story

It started with a missed 3pm slump.

Cadence began as a side project in 2024, after one of our founders kept noticing the same pattern in her own mood-tracking spreadsheet: a dependable dip every day around 3pm, invisible to any of the habit apps she'd tried. None of them were built to notice a rhythm — they were built to enforce a streak.

So we set out to build something smaller and quieter: a companion that checks in three times a day, at the moments that already exist in most people's routines, and slowly learns what actually helps. No leaderboard, no guilt, no red notification badges. Just three honest questions a day, and a weekly note that tells you the truth about your own patterns.

Today Cadence is used by students, shift workers, new parents, and founders across 11 countries — a small, deliberately narrow tool built by a small, deliberately narrow team.

What we believe

A few principles that shape every feature we ship.

01

Fit the day, don't fight it

We design around the checkpoints a day already has, instead of asking people to build new rituals from scratch.

02

No shame in the loop

Missed a day? Nothing resets. Guilt doesn't build habits — noticing does.

03

Your data stays yours

We don't sell check-in data, and we never will. Export or delete it whenever you like.
